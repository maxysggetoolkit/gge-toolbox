🍖 Food Optimisation

Optimisation Economy Food ~5 min read

Food caps your army size: every troop and tool in a castle eats food per hour, and your net food (production − consumption) decides whether you can keep recruiting — and, since the Relic Brewery converts food + honey into mead, your food base also feeds your sovereign troops indirectly. This is the optimisation checklist, in priority order.

Buildings — the structural base

  • 2× Relic Greenhouse per castle → level 20. The backbone of modern food production. Get both built in every castle and levelled.
  • Granaries → level 30.
  • Relic Conservatory ×2 per castle — only worth levelling if you spend big; otherwise leave at low level.
  • Flour Mill → level 6 per castle (construction-token building).
  • Imperial Hunting Lodge: set it to max food. Level 1 is fine — don't waste resources upgrading it.
  • Delete buildings you don't need and buy/level the districts (military, trade), moving the biggest buildings in first — every freed plot is deco space.

Build items — where the big numbers live

  • Target: 10 food build items per castle — 4 across the relic greenhouses/conservatories, 6 on the granaries.
  • Place your best primary items on the relic greenhouse first (lv 20 or your highest), then on the highest-level relic conservatory.
  • The 1.1k relic-slot food items on all granaries and conservatories are the efficient sweet spot; buy a level-30 relic slot item from time to time as upgrades. Level 1 items are fine to start — they're cheap and still significant.
  • Temp food item on woodcutters exists (+food for 4 days) but it's risky and painful to manage — last resort only, when castles are completely full.

Boosts — cheap, permanent or weekly

  • Food Overseer: 157 rubies/week (Kings Market). Cheapest production boost in the game — keep it running, set a reminder so it doesn't lapse.
  • Research: Three-Field Crop Rotation lv 5 = +20% food (Hall of Legends).
  • Coat of arms / banners: 15–20% food variants are viable (the 20% mead emblem is the premium pick if you run sovereign troops — see mead optimisation).
  • VIP level 10 — keep it active.
  • Subscription (optional): the level-1 tier's extra food is the only one worth considering.
  • Food villages: always hold the maximum you can, and over time switch to max-level private food villages — they give the highest bonus and can't be stolen.

Decorations & space

  • Public-order decos drive everything. Use the highest-PO decorations you own — the golden rule is that your best decos should never sit in inventory.
  • Place them where they cover the most production: if your production buildings are concentrated in outposts, the same deco does more work there than in the main castle.
  • Unlock Hall expansions and expand your castles — more room for decorations. Berimond titles add more again.

What the end-game looks like

  • High-PO decos + 1.1k relic-slot items on all granaries and conservatories gets you to 120–150k food per hour comfortably.
  • Built-out kingdoms — level 35 buildings, resource villages, 3.5k-PO decos and the 1.1k relic items — push 170k+ food per hour, at which point kingdom castles out-produce outposts for many players.
  • Running a controlled negative net food off a full storehouse during events is a legitimate tactic (e.g. refilling via Robber Baron loot between hits) — just watch the buffer.

Priority order if starting from zero: greenhouses + granaries → food overseer + research → build items on granaries → PO decos & space → private food villages → conservatories/whale items.
