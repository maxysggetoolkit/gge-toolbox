⚔️ Combat Basics

Beginner Combat Attack Defence ~5 min read

Every battle in GGE is decided by three positions, two unit types, and whose bonuses are bigger. Once that clicks, the rest is detail.

The three positions

A castle is attacked across three fronts. Your wave is split across them:

  • Front — the gate. Usually the most heavily defended.
  • Left flank and Right flank — the two sides.

Defenders sit on the walls of each position; attackers assign units and tools to each. Beat the defenders on a position and your units pour through to the courtyard, where the final fight happens.

Melee vs ranged

Every unit is either melee or ranged. Bonuses are tracked separately for each type and each position. A "+50% ranged front attack" does nothing for your melee units or your flank units. This is why gear and commander stats matter so much — you're stacking the right bonus on the right position and type.

The matchup that wins: your attack strength (for that position + unit type) vs the defender's defence strength (same position + type), each lifted by their bonuses. Bring the unit type the defence is weakest against.

Waves & tools

An attack is one or more waves. Each wave carries units plus tools:

  • Attack tools (e.g. ladders, rams, mantlets) boost your units or weaken a position's defence.
  • Defence tools do the reverse for the castle owner.

Early waves can be cheap "softening" waves that burn down defensive tools and wall bonuses; later waves bring your real army into a weakened castle.

Glory, the wall & the courtyard

Defence comes in layers: wall and gate protection reduce incoming damage, then moat, then the courtyard stand. An attacker wants to out-scale all of it; a defender wants enough wall/gate bonus and courtyard strength that attacks bounce.

Detection matters. Defenders can see incoming attacks after a delay. The later they detect, the less time they have to send reinforcements or swap their castellan. Speed and detection timing is a real lever — not just flavour.
