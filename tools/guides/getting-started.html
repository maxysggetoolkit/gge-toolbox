🚀 Getting Started

Beginner Economy First week ~4 min read

Goodgame Empire throws a hundred buttons at you in the first hour. This is the short version of what actually matters in your first week — and what you can safely ignore.

The core loop

Everything in GGE comes back to four things: produce resources (wood, stone, food), build & upgrade, recruit troops, and fight (NPCs, events, other players). Your castle level and might climb as you do all four.

First-week priorities

  1. Follow the quests. The early quest line is a hand-built tutorial that pays well. Do it before anything else — the rewards fund everything below.
  2. Keep building queues busy. A construction slot sitting idle is wasted time. Always have something upgrading.
  3. Upgrade resource buildings & storehouses. Farms, woodcutters, quarries first; then the storehouse so raids can't empty you.
  4. Build a Keep and Dwelling levels for population. Population caps how much you can build and produce.
  5. Don't over-recruit early. Troops eat food. A starving castle stops growing. Match army size to your food surplus.
Rule of thumb: if a decision is between "more production" and "more troops" in week one, pick production. You can always recruit later; a weak economy holds you back for months.

Resources, briefly

ResourceWhat it's for
Wood & StoneBuilding and upgrading everything.
FoodFeeds your troops. Net food must stay positive.
CoinsRecruiting, research, and tools. Earned from taxes & raiding.
RubiesPremium currency. Hoard them. See below.

What to do with rubies

Rubies are the premium currency and the single easiest thing to waste. The golden rule: rubies buy value, not convenience. Spend them where the return is huge and save them everywhere else.

Good ruby spends:

  • High-value offers — the special / inbox offers (and event shop deals) give far more per ruby than anything you can buy directly. This is where most of your rubies should go.
  • Premium tools — the strong attack and defence tools that win the fights that actually matter. Worth it on a key hit or defence, not on farming.
  • Key unlocks — the occasional building or feature unlock that permanently moves your account forward.
Don't buy extra construction slots with rubies. Time skips show up constantly — from events, quests, daily login and the like — so you rarely wait on a build anyway. A bought slot is a one-off; the rubies are better banked for an offer. Likewise, skip instant-finishing buildings and buying raw resources — both are poor value.

Things you can ignore at first

  • Deep equipment optimisation — your starter gear is fine for now.
  • Alliance wars and PvP tooling — learn the economy first.
  • Min-maxing castle layout — a "good enough" layout beats analysis paralysis.
Join an active alliance early. It's the single biggest quality-of-life upgrade: shared protection, help with construction, and people to answer questions. Pick one that's actually online, not just the biggest.
