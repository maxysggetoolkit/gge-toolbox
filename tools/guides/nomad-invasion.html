🌊 Nomad Invasion

Event PvE Alliance Khan camp ~6 min read

The Nomad Invasion is the Khan's recurring raid on the realm — nomad camps spawn across the world map, and your alliance enrages a shared Khan camp until someone taunts the Khan into attacking. It pays out on two fronts at once: you loot Nomad tablets by clearing camps, and the far more valuable Khan tablets by surviving the Khan's counter-attack. Spend both at the Herald's trader.

Two currencies, one trader. Nomad tablets come from attacking camps; Khan tablets come from defending the taunted Khan. For your event score they are not worth the same — a Khan tablet is worth 7× a Nomad tablet (see Scoring).

The loop

  1. Attack nomad camps on the map → loot Nomad tablets and build rage.
  2. Rage levels up your alliance's shared Khan camp — higher level, bigger payouts.
  3. When the Khan camp is fully enraged, taunt the Khan to trigger an attack on your own main castle.
  4. Defend successfully → loot Khan tablets (the 7× currency) and yet more rage.
  5. Spend both currencies at the Herald of the Invasion trader.

Nomad camps & difficulty levels

Camps come in 8 difficulty tiers. They're pure PvE — every camp of a given tier fields the same garrison of Lancers (melee) and Spear throwers (ranged) behind wall and gate bonuses, so once you've built the attack that clears a tier cleanly, you just repeat it. Defence strength climbs steeply through tier 5, then plateaus while the tablet payout and guard count keep rising — the top tiers aren't much harder to break, they just give more.

The Khan camp (rage & taunt)

The Khan camp is a single, alliance-shared objective on the map — not a personal one. Every nomad camp your alliance clears generates rage, and rage fills the Khan camp's level bar ("X / Y rage to level up"). The higher the Khan camp's level, the tougher the Khan's attack — and the better the loot for surviving it.

  • Enrage it together. Rage is pooled across the alliance, so the whole roster hammering camps is what pushes the Khan camp up its levels.
  • Taunt when full. Once the camp is fully enraged, any member can Taunt the Khan to trigger a Khan attack on their own main castle. You can let the rest of the alliance see the incoming attack so they can rally support.
  • Defend to cash in. Beating the Khan's wave is where the Khan tablets (and more rage) come from. This is the high-value half of the event — it's worth holding troops back to tank these cleanly rather than throwing everything at camps.
  • Cooldown. There's a wait between Khan-camp attacks, and the camp can level up mid-fight, so time your taunts around your defensive readiness.

Scoring — why Khan tablets are worth 7×

Your event rank is driven by a single points total, and the two currencies feed it at very different rates:

SourceCurrencyPoints each
Clearing nomad campsNomad tablet1
Defending the taunted KhanKhan tablet7

So a single Khan tablet moves your score as much as seven Nomad tablets. Camps are the steady grind that fuels rage; the real climb comes from defending Khan attacks. If you're chasing a bracket, prioritise being ready to taunt-and-hold the moment the Khan camp fills.

The trader — Herald of the Invasion

The Herald of the Invasion stands outside your castle and runs the event shop. Both currencies are spent here:

  • Nomad tablets buy the bulk stock — event tools, resources, lower-tier decorations and consumables. It's your high-volume currency, so it clears out the everyday items.
  • Khan tablets buy the headline rewards — event-exclusive equipment, the top troops and decorations, the Pathfinder's Seal (the nomad advisor token) and the event commander. Because they're rarer and score 7×, spend them deliberately.
  • It lingers after the event. The trader stays open for a short while once the invasion ends, and any tablets you don't spend carry over to the next Nomad Invasion — so it's fine to bank toward a big-ticket item across two runs.

Exact stock and prices rotate between runs; check the Event Rewards overview (filter to Nomad Invasion) for the current milestone track.

Farming camps efficiently

  1. Scout once, template forever. Every camp in a tier is identical — Lancers + Spear throwers behind wall/gate bonuses. Build the cheapest clean clear for that tier and copy it.
  2. Bring both lanes + the right siege. A melee/ranged mix plus a ram for the gate and ladders for the wall — the same logic as the PvP tooling guide — beats spamming raw troops into a wall bonus.
  3. Don't over-tool. Tablets scale with the camp's tier, not with how hard you hit it, so a lean clear is more efficient than an overkill.
  4. Skip when it's worth it. Camps have a ruby skip cost if you'd rather buy the clear than march on it — handy on the low tiers when you only want the rage.

Holding the Khan's attack

The Khan's wave is a predictable PvE hit that scales with your alliance's Khan-camp level. A solid castellan setup plus standard wall/courtyard tooling tanks it and banks the Khan tablets. Top up your courtyard and wall capacity before you taunt so more defenders actually fight, and use the Defence Survival calculator to check you'll hold before you enrage the Khan.

Is it worth it?

  • F2P: one of the steadiest sources of event gear, decos and the commander — camps cost mostly troop time, and the defence half is pure value.
  • Spenders: the 7× Khan tablets reward focused taunt-and-defend pushes, but the event recurs often, so there's no need to whale a single run — bank tablets across two.
