🌊 Nomad Invasion

Event PvE Khan medals Weekly ~5 min read

The Nomad Invasion is one of the most reliable point-farms in the game — a recurring PvE event where the Khan's nomads camp across the world map and march on your castle. Clear camps, hold the waves, bank the points, and spend your Khan medals on event-exclusive units, gear and decorations.

How it runs

Nomad Invasion rotates in roughly every week (often a short two-to-three day window). There are two ways to score event points, and you'll want both:

  • Attack nomad camps — camps spawn on the world map at a range of levels. You send attacks against their garrison and earn points per clear, scaling with camp level.
  • Defend your castle — the nomads also send attack waves at you. Surviving (or minimising losses on) those waves banks points too, so a solid castellan setup earns while you sleep.

Points, brackets & rewards

Your points feed a single milestone track. Reward tiers are split by level bracket (the brackets run roughly 70–369, 370–719, 720–1019 and 1020), so two players on very different accounts chase different tables. The track pays out in Silver and Gold pieces along the way, with the headline prize — currently the Holy Nomad hero (a turtle-themed event commander) — sitting near the top.

Separately, clearing camps and hitting tiers awards Khan medals, the event currency. Spend them at the Khan's tent on event-exclusive troops, tools, equipment and decos.

The full, data-backed milestone list per bracket is in the Event Rewards overview — filter to Nomad Invasion.

Farming camps efficiently

  1. It's PvE — you're hitting fixed nomad garrisons, not players. Scout a camp once, build the attack that clears it for the least loss, then repeat that template.
  2. Match the defenders — bring the unit type and tools that counter what the camp fields. Cheap, fast units keep your cost-per-point low.
  3. Use your commander's "every 2/3 waves" skills — the same wave-timing logic from the PvP tooling guide applies: your strongest waves should land where the camp is toughest.
  4. Don't over-tool — points scale with camp level, not with how much you spend, so a lean clear beats an over-killed one when you're grinding the track.

Holding the defensive waves

Incoming nomad waves are predictable PvE hits. A good castellan setup plus standard wall/courtyard tooling will tank most of them and bank defence points. If you're chasing a high bracket, top up your courtyard and wall capacity before the event so more defenders actually fight.

Is it worth it?

  • F2P: yes — it's one of the steadiest sources of Khan-shop gear and the event hero, for not much more than troop time.
  • Spenders: the higher brackets and the hero reward reward a focused push, but the event runs often enough that there's no need to whale a single instance.
