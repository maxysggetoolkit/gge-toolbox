🌅 Beyond the Horizon

Event Temp server Alliance Strategy ~12 min read

Beyond the Horizon drops your team onto a fresh temporary server — the Tangled Wilderness — to fight the world for influence. The reward is the game's six BTH materials, which buy the strongest economy and PvP build items there are. Here's how the event works and the playbook for a big first day.

How it works

You need a level 30+ castle and an alliance, and you must enter within the first 48 hours. Players group into Horizon alliances of 22 — the 23rd member from your alliance opens a second team, the 45th a third. Each Horizon alliance has one Capital, tied to the first member who entered.

Everyone starts with 25,000 influence. Influence you gather during the day transfers to your Capital at the daily reset (0:30 CET / 6:30pm EST) — and once banked it can't be plundered. Final alliance ranking is the influence left in the vault when the event ends.

Day-1 playbook

Times below are EST (= CET − 6h). The exact prime-time schedule shifts between editions — check yours in-game on day 1.

  1. Jump early (~5:40am EST), capital holder first — you land under 6 hours of bird protection.
  2. First 200% prime time (~6:30am): the three ruby packages (≈$50 total) give roughly 1.6M food, 1.6M coins, 1.4M wood, 1.4M stone, 14k vet demons and 14k vet defenders.
  3. Shopping care offer: take all XPs if pushing level 70 — take none if staying low.
  4. Under bird protection: expand walls, max towers, upgrade the Keep, Bakers, Stable and Watchtower; build 2 relic greenhouses (construction/upgrade tokens come from the packages, the special inbox offer and the armourer); add guardhouses and farmhouses. Skip wood and stone buildings entirely.
  5. Commanders & castellans: 2× Legacy of the Cult commanders from the equipment trader (65k coins each); the 53k-ruby relic castellan if budget allows (the 29k one otherwise); Son of the North gems — 2× courtyard, 2× wall troops. Watch your inbox each morning for the once-a-day relic commander look pieces offer.
  6. Food discipline: if you're doing cap slowdown hits, delete ~80% of your vet demons on day 1 and recruit macemen instead. Research Craftsmanship 3 → Plow 3, hire the food overseer (650 rubies / 7 days), and buy Maps from the Kings Market so troops return faster.

Cap slowdown hits — the points engine

Influence transfers to capitals at the daily reset. Hitting an enemy capital at reset + 1 second with cheap troops "slows" the transfer and steals points. This is where the big day-1 scores come from.

  • Scout targets early: check the command-point rankings (roughly top 30–330) for capitals that won't be defended after reset but whose members hold good might and command points. City States add command points — they're a good tell.
  • Cheap waves: coin troops from the armourer or macemen; presets of 32–50 troops, 2/3 flanks or all-mid. Each hit costs 200 rubies.
  • Volume: days 1–2: 200–300 hits · days 3–4: 200–250 · day 5: 100–150 · day 6: ~100.
Golden tip: press the slowdown button the moment the arrival time ticks over to the next minute. Landing among the first 3 hits instead of 10th–15th is worth ~50% more points at transfer.

The goal: top 400 individual — that takes roughly 4–5M points. Day 1 should bank most of it via slowdown hits (20–40k rubies of hits); days 2–6 aim for 800k–1M per day. Not buying rubies? Grind capitals of fresh low-level players still holding their 25k starting influence — many are undefended and VIP commanders are enough. Low-level players can ride City State maturation instead of slowdown hits. The 15% capital boost (100k rubies from the alliance treasury, 24h) is good value on days 1–3.

Spending smart

After the day-1 buy, the next good purchase is day 2 or day 4 when the cart offer includes feathers — best combined with the 50k and/or 150k package, ideally during a 200% window. Feathers let you keep hitting castles and capitals right after reset.

What the materials buy

The point of all this: Timber, Straw, Nails, Glue, Cobblestone and Fabric. Back home they craft the strongest build items in the game.

The toolsmith/refinery items boost output directly — a maxed 30% item turns a 200-screw hour into 260 for the same base resource cost. See the Mead Optimisation guide for where the mead and honey items slot into the bigger production chain.

Free mats trick: the master blacksmith's premium relic food widgets dismantle into BTH materials. Buying the 15k-gold widget twice (40 fabric) beats the 25k widget (30 fabric). Fill all castles with L30 widgets before burning gold on this.

Defending in BTH

Offline setup: 99-1-1 or 1-1-99 (everything on one flank), tooled 45 deep with 4 slits and fire moats, wall at 60 melee / 40 range.
